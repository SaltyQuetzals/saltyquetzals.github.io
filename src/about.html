About Me

I got my bachelor’s degree in computer science from Texas A&M, and like to believe that my degree gave me the technical chops to build solutions that help people. But I wasn’t sure how to identify what problems people needed solved, so I’m getting my Master’s in Human-Computer Interaction at Georgia Tech to:

  • learn how to identify real problems that real people have, and build cool things to solve them
  • evaluate those solutions to see if they’re as useful as people need them to be, and
  • avoid being an adult for a couple more years.

I’d love for this conversation to be a bit less one-sided, so feel free to reach out to me!
